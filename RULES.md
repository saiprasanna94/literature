# Literature (Fish) — Game Rules

Literature (also known as Fish) is a high-level strategy card game that relies heavily on memory, deduction, and teamwork. Unlike most card games, it involves no betting or luck of the draw once the cards are dealt — it is purely a game of information.

## 1. The Setup

- **Players:** 6 or 8 people, divided into two equal teams. Teams sit in alternating order: Team A, Team B, Team A, Team B.
- **The Deck:** 54 cards (standard 52 + 2 Jokers).
- **Dealing:** All cards are dealt out face-down.
  - 6-player game: everyone gets 9 cards.
  - 8-player game: some players may have more than others, or the deck can be adjusted.
- **Objective:** Claim more sets than the opposing team. There are 9 sets total; the first team to get 5 sets wins.

## 2. The 9 Sets (Books)

Each "set" (or **Book**) consists of 6 cards.

| Set Name        | Cards Included                              |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Low Hearts      | 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 of Hearts                  |
| High Hearts     | 9, 10, J, Q, K, A of Hearts                 |
| Low Diamonds    | 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 of Diamonds                |
| High Diamonds   | 9, 10, J, Q, K, A of Diamonds               |
| Low Spades      | 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 of Spades                  |
| High Spades     | 9, 10, J, Q, K, A of Spades                 |
| Low Clubs       | 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 of Clubs                   |
| High Clubs      | 9, 10, J, Q, K, A of Clubs                  |
| The 8s (Jokers) | All four 8s + Big Joker + Small Joker       |

## 3. Gameplay Mechanics

### Asking for Cards

On your turn, you may ask any player on the opposing team for a specific card. Two rules apply:

1. **Possession:** You must already hold at least one card from that set to ask for others in it.
2. **Specificity:** You must ask for a specific card (e.g., "Team B Player 1, do you have the 6 of Hearts?").

### Turns

- **Success:** If the person has the card, they must give it to you. You continue your turn and can ask anyone from the opposing team for another card.
- **Failure:** If they do not have the card, your turn ends. The person who was just asked now takes the turn for their team.

> You can never ask your own teammate for a card, but you must listen to what they ask others to deduce what they are holding.

## 4. Claiming a Set (Literature)

When a player believes their team holds all 6 cards of a set, they can **Claim** it. This can be done during their turn.

- The player announces the set they are claiming.
- They must state exactly who on their team has which card (e.g., "I have the 2 and 3, Sarah has the 4 and 7, and Mike has the 5 and 6").

**Result:**

- **If correct:** The team wins the set.
- **If incorrect:** The opposing team gets the set (even if they didn't have all the cards).

## 4a. Turn Transitions (precise)

A new turn is assigned only in these cases:

| Event          | Who gets the next turn                                                                  |
| -------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Ask succeeds   | Asker keeps the turn                                                                    |
| Ask fails      | The asked player (opposing team) gets the turn directly                                 |
| Claim succeeds | Any non-empty player on the claimer's team — including the claimer — may take over (first to act) |
| Claim fails    | Opposing team wins the set; any non-empty player on the opposing team may take over (first to act) |

**Empty-handed players are fully passive:** they cannot ask, cannot be asked, and cannot be passed a turn.

## 4b. Ask Restrictions (precise)

- Target must be on the **opposing team** — never yourself, never a teammate.
- Target must have **at least 1 card** in hand.
- Asker must hold **at least 1 card from the same set** as the requested card.
- Asker must **not** ask for a card they themselves hold (the UI prevents this).

## 5. Key Rules & Strategy

- **Memory is Everything:** You must remember who asked for what. If a teammate asks for the 4 of Spades, you now know they have at least one card in the Low Spades set.
- **The "Eights" Strategy:** Since the 8s and Jokers form a special set, players often use these as "safe" asks to figure out where the power lies.
- **No "Table Talk":** You cannot discuss your hand with teammates. All communication must happen through the legal "asks" made during the game.
- **Empty Hands:** If a player runs out of cards (because their cards were taken or they claimed a set), they no longer participate in the "asking", but their team continues until all sets are claimed.

## 6. Winning the Game

Once all 9 sets have been claimed, the team with the most sets (minimum 5) is declared the winner. If a team claims 5 sets early, the game usually ends there.
